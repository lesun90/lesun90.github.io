I currently work as a motion planning engineer at American Haval Moto Technology in Farmington Hills, MI. My work focuses on enhancing the capability of our company autonomous driving system, including research, develop and implement motion planning algorithms (in simulation and deployment to real vehicle) for various scenarios and enviroments, also perform tasks of analyze data to assess and improve system performance.

In 2018, I obtained my Ph.D. degree in Computer Science at The Catholic University of America (Washington, DC) under the direction of Dr. Erion Plaku. My Ph.D. research focuses on task and motion planning autonomous robotic system (e.g: autonomous vehicle) which enable robotic systems to safely and effectively accomplish sophisticated tasks.
