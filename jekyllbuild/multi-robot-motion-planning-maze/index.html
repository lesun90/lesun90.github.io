I am Duong Le. I work on simulated and real-time on-road motion planning systems for robotics and autonomous driving, in particular, my expertise is in motion planning, robotic simulation, and programming.

Now I am a motion planning engineer at American Haval Motor Technology (R&D Centre of Great Wall Motor Company). I design, develop, and implement motion planning algorithms in simulation and physical deployment for edge cases, also analyze log data for root cause and improve system performance.

I received my Ph.D. degree in Computer Science at The Catholic University of America with Dr. Erion Plaku in 2018. During my Ph.D. study, I focus on task and motion planning autonomous robotic system which enables robotic systems to safely and effectively accomplish sophisticated tasks.

Multi-Robot Sampling-Based Motion Planning with Dynamics [maze scene]

Posted by • Published on January 02, 2018

This project develops an effective multi-robot motion planner. The approach takes into account geometric and differential constraints imposed by the obstacles and the robot dynamics by using sampling to expand a motion tree in the composite state space of all the robots.
